Rocky Mountain High - Population Growth in the Mountain States (2000-2017)

Map authored by Tim Kohaya for NewMaps Plus Course 672

About this Map

This map shows the percentage of population growth for counties and cities in the Mountain States of the U.S. between 2000 and 2017. Cities shown on the map had a population of at least 30,000 in 2000. You can hover over a County and see the county's population in 2000 and 2017 and the percent change. You can also click on a city and see the same information. The cities layer toggles on and off. Data from 2000 is a population count (2000 Census) while data from 2017 is a population estimate (2012-2017 American Fact Finder).

What the Data Shows

Many counties of the Mountain States have seen large population growth between 2000 and 2017. Counties in scenic or tourist areas such as those in western Colorado, western Montana and central New Mexico have high percentages of growth. Counties with traditional retirement areas such as those in Arizona and southern Nevada also have high percentages of growth.

Like many counties in the Mountain States, many cities in the Mountain States have also seen large population growth. Major cities such as Phoenix, Las Vegas and Denver have seen growth but it is their suburbs that have the highest percentages of growth. Smaller cities in scenic or tourist areas have also have seen a lot of population growth.

Data Sources

County population data layer produced by joining data downloaded from American Fact Finder with U.S. County polygons downloaded from U.S. Boundary Shapefiles.

City population data layer produced by joining data downloaded from MapCruzin with U.S. County polygons downloaded from U.S. Boundary Shapefiles.

State boundaries downloaded from U.S. Boundary Shapefiles. All data prepared by U.S. Census Bureau, 2017.
